Albert Georgijev

My father was known by most as 'Fred'. 'Alfred' according to his British passport. However, his birth name was 'Albert' and he was born in Niitskovitsa village, Ingria, Russia. A touch confusing?
A deep sadness in his past he kept to himself: a past where his family were stripped of all they owned and made homeless. The change that Stalin brought affected the lives of millions and for those specifically targeted normal life came to an end. There seemed no place for the likes of the Georgijev family in Stalin's Russia. The government programme of class genocide was carried out ruthlessly by an army of secret police (NKVD )whose agents were accountable only to the highest office.
My father's parents, Vassili and Alide, plus five children were left homeless and unwanted.
